# Atelier token-lever benchmark — fair-test re-run (2026-06-20)

Over-build-prone tasks + haiku & sonnet — the decisive per-worker run.

3 over-build-prone tasks (date-field, file-dropzone, form-validation) x 4 arms x
{haiku, sonnet} x 2 reps = 48 cells, 47 completed (1 timeout), 600s cap.

## Verdicts (now decisive)

**terse (`_TERSE_OUTPUT_RULE`, the caveman lever) — NET LOSS, model-dependent harm.**
On haiku: **+99% cost, +143% wall-time, AND lower correctness (2.17 vs 2.50)** — it cut
LOC but made the small model deliberate ~2.4x longer, cost ~2x, and answer worse. On
sonnet it's ~neutral (-12% cost). This matches ponytail exactly: terse counter-
instructions make weaker models overthink and fail more. The always-on terse rule is
hurting cheap-tier (haiku) dispatches the most — and atelier routes mechanical phases
to haiku.

**minimal_diff (`_MINIMAL_DIFF_RULE`) — a real, modest WIN.** It drives the over-
engineering judge to ~0 (haiku 0.50 -> 0.00) and cuts cost (haiku -8%, sonnet -22%),
at a small correctness cost (~-0.15: haiku 2.50->2.33, sonnet 2.67->2.50). The lever
does what it was designed to do — less over-build, lower cost — with a completeness
tradeoff worth watching. Safety held (safe_rate 1.0 in the prior run).

**both — cheapest overall (haiku -10%, sonnet -23%), correctness intact.** minimal_diff's
brevity OFFSETS terse's deliberation blowup: terse-alone on haiku is $0.117, but
terse+minimal_diff is $0.053. So the danger zone is **terse WITHOUT minimal_diff** —
which is exactly what NON-implementation phases (design/review) get, since minimal_diff
is phase-gated to impl phases only.

## Production recommendations (now evidence-backed)
1. **The always-on terse rule is the suspect lever.** It's a net loss alone, worst on
   haiku + on non-impl phases (where minimal_diff doesn't offset it). Gate it OFF and
   A/B whole-cycle; strongly consider removing it entirely for haiku-tier / non-impl
   dispatches. *(Outcome: B1 was subsequently deleted from production.)*
2. **Keep minimal_diff ON for implementer (tdd) phases** — it earns its place (less
   over-build, lower cost); monitor the small correctness dip.

## Remaining caveat
Even the "over-build-prone" tasks under-triggered over-build — both models mostly chose
native solutions (`<input type=date|file>`, HTML5 validation), so LOC deltas are small
(2.5->1); the signal lives in the over-engineering JUDGE scores + cost/time, not raw LOC.
A harder over-build trap (a task with no native shortcut, e.g. a custom data grid) would
amplify the effect. n=6 per (arm,model) — directional.
